# Privacy Policy — Inkling

_Last updated: [DATE]_

Inkling is built to keep your meetings private. This policy explains, plainly,
what the app does and does not do with your information.

## The short version
Inkling does **not** collect, transmit, sell, or share your data. There are no
accounts, no analytics, no ads, and no third‑party servers. Everything you
create stays on your device and, if you choose, in your own private iCloud.

## What Inkling stores, and where
- **Your notes, transcripts, drawings, photos, and AI wrap‑ups** are stored
  **on your device**.
- If you are signed into iCloud, this content syncs across your own Apple devices
  through your **private iCloud (CloudKit private database)**. It is accessible
  only to you; the developer cannot see it.
- App preferences (appearance, settings) are stored on your device.

## Processing happens on your device
- **Transcription** uses Apple's on‑device speech recognition.
- **Text recognition** (handwriting and photos) uses Apple's on‑device Vision.
- **Summaries, prep, and "Ask Inkling"** use Apple's on‑device foundation models
  (Apple Intelligence).
Your content is **not** sent to the developer or to any third party for any of
these features.

## Information you grant access to
With your permission, Inkling uses:
- **Microphone** — to record and transcribe meetings.
- **Camera / Photos** — to attach a photo of your notes and read its text.
- **Calendar** — to show your events and create events from a note.
- **Reminders** — to save action items you choose to add.
This access is used only to provide these features, on your device. You can
change permissions any time in system Settings.

## Data collection
Inkling collects **no** personal data and uses **no** tracking. Accordingly, its
App Store privacy label is "Data Not Collected."

## Children
Inkling does not collect data from anyone, including children.
